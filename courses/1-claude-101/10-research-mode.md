## Learning objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Describe Research mode and when to use it vs. a standard Claude search
- Conduct multi-source, multi-step investigations using Research mode
- Manage your research appropriately through effective prompting

## Understanding the key capability

Claude with Research mode: Understanding how Research mode can initiate a comprehensive research conversation with Claude that spans multiple sources, taking a synthesized approach.

Research mode is Claude's capability for deep, systematic research that can:
- Search and synthesize information from multiple sources simultaneously
- Run queries continuously to collect information
- Tackle complex research that typically requires hours of manual work
- Gather high-signal information to investigate specific topics with structured analysis

## What is Research?

Research mode can complete any task that would typically require:
- Tracking down multiple sources and synthesizing them manually
- You can quickly, greatly guide this the more information Claude has about your needs

## Why use Research?

- **Comprehensive coverage** — Research mode searches hundreds of sources to find the most relevant information
- **Time savings** — Instead of spending hours gathering information, you can focus on analysis
- **Through investigation** — Research mode can plan and execute multiple follow-up searches automatically
- **Synthesis of findings** — The result is a structured report, not just a list of links

## How Research mode works

1. Turn on Research mode by clicking the globe icon in the chat interface or selecting it from the tool options
2. Claude establishes an approach by formulating multiple research questions
3. Claude runs sequential searches — gathering information, then running follow-up searches based on what it learns
4. Claude synthesizes findings into a structured output

## Using Research responsibly

Research mode is powerful, but like any research tool, its findings should be reviewed:
- **Verify claims** — Claude will cite sources, but it's good practice to verify claims that are critical to your work
- **Check publication dates** — Research mode searches the web, but some information may be out of date
- **Triangulate important findings** — If something is critical, verify it through a second source

## Thinking about investigation topics

Start with a clear objective:
- **Too vague:** "Tell me about market trends"
- **Better:** "Research the top 3 emerging trends in B2B SaaS pricing models in 2024, focusing on usage-based pricing adoption and examples from companies with $10M-$100M ARR"

The more specific your objective, the more targeted and useful Research mode's output will be.
